I earned my first Airbnb Superhost badge about eight months into hosting. I did not plan for it or track a checklist. I just responded to guests quickly, kept the place genuinely clean, and never canceled on anyone. One day the badge appeared. Three years and two units later, I still have it, and I can tell you it matters more than most hosting articles let on.

Here is what Superhost status actually requires, what it does for your listing, and the specific things that trip up new hosts before they ever get there.

## TLDR: Key Takeaways

- You need a 4.8+ overall rating, 90%+ response rate, less than 1% cancellation rate, and at least 10 completed stays in the past year
- Airbnb checks your status automatically, four times a year. You do not apply
- The Superhost badge boosts search visibility and guest confidence in a measurable way
- Losing status is easy if you do not have systems. Regaining it takes a full quarter
- Guest Favorite is a separate badge with different criteria. You can hold both

## The Exact Requirements (No Fluff)

Airbnb evaluates Superhost status every three months. To qualify, you need to meet all four criteria during the past 12-month window:

**4.8 or higher overall rating.** This is your average across all reviews. A single 3-star rating from a difficult guest will not end your streak, but a run of 4.5s will. The math is unforgiving at lower volume. Ten stays means one bad review moves the needle by a full tenth of a point.

**90% or higher response rate.** Airbnb measures your response rate on the first message or inquiry within each conversation thread. You need to reply within 24 hours. After that first reply, the clock resets. Most hosts lose points here not because they ignore guests, but because they miss inquiry notifications during work hours or overnight.

**Less than 1% cancellation rate.** For most hosts doing 10-30 stays per year, this means zero host-initiated cancellations. One cancellation at low volume puts you at 3-10%, which disqualifies you immediately. Airbnb does make exceptions for Major Disruptive Events, but standard situations like a plumbing issue or a schedule conflict do not qualify.

**At least 10 completed reservations, or 3 reservations totaling 100+ nights.** New hosts often do not realize the 100-night alternative exists. If you host longer stays, three reservations of 34 nights each would qualify you even if you have never hosted a short weekend trip.

Airbnb's quarterly review dates fall in January, April, July, and October. If you miss a requirement, you lose the badge at the next review. You can regain it the following quarter if you meet the criteria again.

## What the Superhost Badge Actually Does for Your Listing

The badge is not just a trophy. There are real, measurable effects on your listing performance.

**Search visibility.** Superhost listings appear higher in Airbnb search results, all else being equal. In competitive markets, this alone can be the difference between consistent bookings and sitting empty on weekends.

**Guest trust.** Airbnb's own data shows that 59% of guests say Superhost status makes them more confident in the quality of the accommodation. That confidence translates directly to click-through and booking rates, especially from guests who are new to Airbnb or unfamiliar with your area.

**Earnings lift.** Superhosts earn roughly 60% more than non-Superhost listings, according to Airbnb. Some of that gap comes from higher nightly rates, some from better occupancy. Both improve when guests filter for Superhost listings, which many experienced travelers do by default.

**Exclusive perks.** Superhost status comes with a $100 Airbnb travel coupon each year you maintain it, priority access to Airbnb customer support, and early access to new hosting features when Airbnb rolls them out.

## The Four Things New Hosts Get Wrong

I have talked to a lot of new hosts who were convinced they were on track for Superhost status and then missed the quarterly review. The mistakes tend to fall into four categories.

**Letting the response rate slip.** The 90% threshold sounds easy until you go on vacation, attend a work event, or just have a chaotic week. One missed inquiry that goes unanswered for 24 hours can drop you below the threshold if your volume is low. The fix is to turn on Airbnb app notifications and set up a saved message template so you can fire off a quick acknowledgment even when you are not ready to answer fully. A "Thanks for reaching out, I will get back to you with details shortly" response counts.

**Canceling for fixable reasons.** A guest-friendly host never cancels. If something goes wrong at the property, the right move is to contact Airbnb support and document the issue before taking any action. Airbnb support can sometimes mark a cancellation as extenuating circumstances, which protects your rate. A cancellation you initiate on your own does not get that protection.

**Underestimating the rating math.** At 10 stays, a single 3-star review drops your average by roughly 0.18 points if your other nine stays are all 5-star. That might push a 4.9 host down to 4.72, below the threshold. The practical lesson: focus hardest on hospitality during your first 20 stays, because every review counts more when your total is small.

**Confusing Guest Favorite with Superhost.** These are two different badges with different criteria. Guest Favorite is based on your reviews, ratings, and reliability score across all your stays on Airbnb, using a slightly different formula. You can hold both badges at the same time. I currently do at my main house. The Guest Favorite badge shows up directly on your listing page, while Superhost shows on your profile. Both help, but they come from different places.

## The Systems That Keep You from Losing It

Reaching Superhost status once is the easy part. The hosts who keep it year after year do so because they run on systems rather than memory.

**Automated messaging.** Set up scheduled messages in your Airbnb hosting tools for check-in instructions, a mid-stay check-in, and checkout reminders. When guests feel informed and supported, they rate you higher. When you stop relying on yourself to remember every message, you stop making the errors that hurt ratings.

**A pre-departure walkthrough system.** I do a quick visual check between every stay. I use a physical checklist so nothing gets missed when I am tired or in a hurry. A detailed guest guidebook helps here too, because guests who know how everything works leave fewer complaints in reviews. I put together [The Complete Airbnb Guidebook template](https://shanadesigns.gumroad.com/l/airbnb-guidebook-template) to make that process faster. It covers the property info, house rules, local recommendations, and checkout instructions in one place that guests actually read.

**A reliable cleaning process.** Most bad ratings come from cleanliness issues. A documented cleaning checklist your team follows the same way every single time is not optional if you want a 4.8. Read more about building that process in our [Airbnb cleaning checklist guide](/blog/airbnb-cleaning-checklist).

**Calendar discipline.** Keep your calendar updated. Hosting a stay on dates when your calendar is blocked out as available, only to scramble and cancel, is exactly the scenario that kills Superhost status in one move. If you need dates off, block them well in advance.

## Should You Chase Superhost Status?

If you are hosting more than a few times a year and treating it like a business, yes. The search visibility and guest trust effects are real, and the quarterly review keeps you accountable to the kind of hospitality that generates repeat guests and strong word-of-mouth.

If you are hosting occasionally and do not want to commit to the 90% response rate or the consistent calendar management, the pressure of chasing Superhost status may not be worth it for your situation.

For most hosts I have talked to, though, the habits required to become a Superhost are the same habits that make the whole business run better: fast communication, reliable cleaning, no cancellations, and guests who feel genuinely taken care of. The badge just follows naturally from doing the work.

Track your progress anytime by going to your Airbnb stats dashboard. Airbnb shows you exactly where you stand on each metric ahead of the next quarterly review. If you are close on response rate or rating, you will know exactly what to focus on.

The badge will come. Keep hosting like it matters, because it does.
