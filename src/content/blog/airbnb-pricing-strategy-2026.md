## Why Your Pricing Strategy Is More Than a Number

Set your price too low and you fill every night — but your revenue stays flat and guests assume there is a catch. Set it too high and your calendar sits empty while your competitors collect bookings. The real goal of an Airbnb pricing strategy is to hit the optimal nightly rate for every specific date on your calendar.

That requires three things: a solid base price, a dynamic adjustment layer, and manual overrides for high-demand dates. This guide covers all three.

---

## The Three Airbnb Pricing Models

### Static Pricing

You set one price and it applies to every night unless you manually change it. Simple, but leaves significant revenue on the table during demand spikes and overprices your listing during slow periods. Most new hosts start here by default.

### Airbnb Smart Pricing

Airbnb's built-in tool adjusts your nightly rate automatically based on its own demand data. The problem: Smart Pricing optimizes for bookings, not revenue. Experienced hosts consistently report that it pushes prices to the minimum floor they set, particularly during slow periods. It fills your calendar but often at rates well below market value. [Airbnb Help Center](https://www.airbnb.com/resources/hosting-homes/a/setting-your-pricing-strategy-15)

### Third-Party Dynamic Pricing

Tools like PriceLabs, Beyond Pricing, and Wheelhouse use larger market datasets, competitor tracking, local event detection, and booking pace signals to set rates that maximize revenue — not just occupancy. A 2025 study tracking 541 Airbnb listings across 34 countries found a **36% revenue increase** after hosts switched from static to dynamic pricing. Industry benchmarks put the annual improvement at 20-40%. [StaySTRA 2026 Dynamic Pricing Study](https://staystra.com/pricelabs-vs-wheelhouse-vs-beyond-pricing-2026/)

The consensus among high-earning hosts: avoid Smart Pricing for rate-setting, use a third-party tool, and apply manual overrides for local events.

## How to Set Your Base Price

Your base price is the default nightly rate that applies before any dynamic adjustments. Getting it right matters more than most hosts realize — it acts as the anchor for everything else.

**Step 1: Research your actual comp set.** Search Airbnb for listings that match yours in location, size, and amenity level. Filter for upcoming dates 3-4 weeks out and note what comparable properties charge mid-week versus weekends. This is your market floor and ceiling.

**Step 2: Calculate your cost floor.** Add up your monthly fixed costs (mortgage or rent allocation, insurance, utilities, supplies) and divide by your target occupied nights. This is the absolute minimum price that keeps you profitable — never go below it.

**Step 3: Set minimum and maximum price guardrails.** Whatever dynamic pricing tool you use, set a minimum that covers your costs with a margin, and a maximum that reflects what top listings in your market actually command. Without guardrails, automated tools can underprice on slow days or overprice during moderate demand, causing you to lose bookings that would have been profitable. [Guesty Airbnb Pricing Guide](https://www.guesty.com/blog/airbnb-pricing-strategy/)

**Step 4: Revisit quarterly.** Market conditions shift. New competitors come in, neighborhood demand changes, and your own review count grows (which directly affects how much guests will pay). A price that was right six months ago may be 15% off today.

## What Is a Good Airbnb Occupancy Rate?

The industry benchmark for a well-performing listing is **55% occupancy or above**. But occupancy rate alone is a misleading metric — a host at 90% occupancy might earn less than one at 65% occupancy if the latter is charging 40% more per night.

What matters is Revenue Per Available Night (RevPAN): your average nightly rate multiplied by your occupancy rate. A listing earning $150/night at 60% occupancy ($90 RevPAN) outperforms one earning $100/night at 80% occupancy ($80 RevPAN).

Occupancy rate also varies significantly by market, property type, and seasonality. Urban markets often run 50-70% annual occupancy. Beachside and resort markets can hit 75-90% during peak months but dip below 40% in the off-season. [Mashvisor Airbnb Occupancy Rate Guide](https://www.mashvisor.com/blog/airbnb-occupancy-rate/) [AirDNA Occupancy Rate Analysis](https://www.airdna.co/blog/airbnb-hosting-tips-for-occupancy-in-2023)

The goal: find the price point where RevPAN is highest, not where the calendar is fullest.

## Event and Peak Season Pricing

Local events are the single most underutilized revenue opportunity for most hosts. Concerts, sporting events, marathons, conferences, and major holidays all spike demand — and most hosts either miss them entirely or apply only modest price increases.

**The framework for event pricing:**

1. **Find upcoming events.** Google "[your city] events calendar [month]" and check local convention center and venue sites. Tools like PriceLabs and Wheelhouse also detect event-based demand automatically.

2. **Raise rates by 50-150% for peak event dates.** The exact amount depends on your market, but major events like championships or multi-day festivals justify rates far above your standard weekend price.

3. **Increase minimum stay requirements.** For a 3-day event, set a 3-night minimum. This fills the full event window and prevents a 1-night booking from blocking a more profitable multi-night stay.

4. **Close gaps around the event.** Single-night gaps before or after an event booking are difficult to fill and disrupt workflow. Adjust minimum stays to eliminate them.

Hosts in Southern California have a massive multi-year opportunity ahead. The FIFA World Cup 2026 LA games run June-July 2026, and LA28 Olympic and Paralympic events span June 15 through September 5, 2028, with 19+ events planned for Long Beach alone. Long Beach hosts can study how real operators are positioning and pricing their properties at [longbeacholympics.com](https://longbeacholympics.com) — a working example of event-optimized Airbnb listing strategy.

For seasonal pricing more broadly: during peak season, raise prices and extend minimum stays. During the off-season, lower prices and shorten minimum stays to capture short-trip demand. In the shoulder season, track local event calendars and adjust weekly. ## The Best Airbnb Pricing Tools in 2026

### PriceLabs — Best for Control and Customization

**Price:** $19.99/listing/month (flat fee)  
**Best for:** Hosts with 3+ properties who want granular control over pricing rules

PriceLabs is the most widely used third-party pricing tool in the short-term rental space. It pulls competitor data, detects local events, tracks your booking pace, and lets you build custom rule sets for minimum stays, discounts, and seasonal adjustments. The flat fee makes it cost-effective as revenue grows.

### Beyond Pricing — Best for Simplicity

**Price:** 1-1.25% of revenue  
**Best for:** New hosts who want automation with minimal setup

Beyond charges a percentage of revenue rather than a flat fee, which keeps costs low for new listings but gets expensive as your nightly rates climb. A listing earning $5,000/month pays $50-62 with Beyond vs. $19.99 with PriceLabs. The trade-off is setup time: Beyond requires almost no configuration. [Rakidzich Pricing Tools Comparison 2026](https://www.rakidzich.com/articles/airbnb-pricing-tools-comparison)

### Wheelhouse — Best for Market Intelligence

**Price:** Free plan available; paid plans from $19.99/listing/month  
**Best for:** Data-focused hosts and multi-market operators

Wheelhouse is the only major dynamic pricing tool with a free tier, which makes it worth trying before committing to a paid plan. Its standout feature is real-time pace tracking — showing whether you are ahead or behind comparable listings in booking velocity for upcoming dates. [StaySTRA Dynamic Pricing Comparison](https://staystra.com/pricelabs-vs-wheelhouse-vs-beyond-pricing-2026/)

## The Bottom Line

Your Airbnb pricing strategy is not a set-it-and-forget-it decision. The hosts who earn the most check their calendar, their competitors, and their local event schedule consistently — and they use the right tools to automate the routine adjustments while staying hands-on for high-value dates.

Start with a solid base price, add a dynamic pricing layer, and pay close attention to your event calendar. Those three habits alone put you ahead of the majority of hosts on the platform.
