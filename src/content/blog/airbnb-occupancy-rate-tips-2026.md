## What Is a Good Airbnb Occupancy Rate?

A good Airbnb occupancy rate is generally 55% or higher for most U.S. markets, with 65-75% considered strong performance. Anything above 75% is exceptional — and can actually signal that your nightly rate is too low.

The 2026 national context matters here. According to [iGMS](https://www.igms.com/airbnb-vacancy-rate/), the U.S. average occupancy rate fell from 57% in 2024 to roughly 50% in spring 2025 as new supply flooded major markets. [Sean Rakidzich](https://www.rakidzich.com/articles/airbnb-occupancy-rate), a 155-property operator, puts the current national average at 54.3% as of mid-2025. That means beating the average is entirely achievable with the right moves.

One important caveat: occupancy rate alone is not a complete performance metric. A listing at 90% occupancy at $80/night earns less than one at 65% occupancy at $180/night. Your goal is to maximize **revenue** — and that means optimizing the relationship between occupancy and nightly rate, not chasing bookings at any price. [AirDNA](https://www.airdna.co/blog/average-occupancy-rate-airbnb) refers to this balance as RevPAR (Revenue Per Available Room/Night), and it is the metric professional hosts track most closely.

## How to Calculate Your Occupancy Rate

The formula is:

> **(Nights Booked / Nights Available) x 100 = Occupancy Rate**

Example: If your listing is available 30 nights in May and books 21 of them, your occupancy rate is 70%. Track this monthly, then compare it against your local market average using tools like [AirDNA](https://www.airdna.co) or [Rabbu](https://rabbu.com). ([Guesty](https://www.guesty.com/blog/airbnb-average-occupancy-rates/))

## 7 Tactics to Increase Your Airbnb Occupancy Rate

### 1. Use Dynamic Pricing

Static pricing is the fastest way to leave money and bookings on the table. Demand on Airbnb shifts by the hour — local events, competitor availability, weather, and booking lead time all affect what guests will pay and when they search.

Dynamic pricing tools adjust your rates automatically based on real-time market data. According to [Beyond Pricing](https://beyondpricing.com/blog/dynamic-pricing-airbnb-complete-guide), property managers using dynamic pricing generate 10-40% more revenue than those on flat rates. [Sean Rakidzich](https://www.rakidzich.com/articles/dynamic-pricing-airbnb-guide) puts the revenue lift at 15-36% compared to static pricing.

The most-used tools among serious hosts are [PriceLabs](https://hello.pricelabs.co) and Beyond Pricing. Both integrate directly with Airbnb and pull local comp data to set your floor, ceiling, and event-based rate adjustments automatically.

**Quick wins with dynamic pricing:**
- Set a base price 10-15% below market average to build initial traction on a new or relaunched listing
- Apply weekend premiums of 20-35% above your weekday base
- Flag local event dates manually (concerts, sports, conventions) to capture demand spikes

### 2. Optimize for the Airbnb Search Algorithm

Airbnb ranks listings based on four primary signals: **quality, popularity, price, and reliability**, according to [Airbnb's own Help Center](https://www.airbnb.com/help/article/39). Your position in search results has a direct effect on how often guests find — and book — your listing.

[Hostaway](https://www.hostaway.com/blog/airbnb-search-algorithm/) breaks down what each signal actually measures:

- **Quality:** Review scores, photo quality, listing completeness, and accurate descriptions
- **Popularity:** Booking frequency, wishlist saves, and page views
- **Price:** Your rate relative to comparable listings on any given date
- **Reliability:** Response rate, cancellation rate, and calendar accuracy

New listings get a short-term visibility boost — Airbnb surfaces them higher in results to generate early booking data. Use that window aggressively: keep your price competitive, respond instantly, and collect your first 5+ reviews as fast as possible. After that boost fades, the algorithm relies entirely on your ongoing performance data. ([Recreation Stays, 2026](https://recreationstays.com/blog/how-airbnb-search-algorithm-works/))

**Practical actions:**
- Maintain a response rate above 90% (aim for under 1-hour response time)
- Keep your calendar up-to-date — blocked and inaccurate dates hurt reliability scores
- Write a title that targets the specific type of traveler your listing suits best
- Add every relevant amenity, even minor ones — each checkbox improves match quality in filtered searches

### 3. Invest in Professional Photos

Photos are the first thing a potential guest sees, and they make or break your click-through rate from search results. [Property Photo Guy](https://propertyphotoguy.co.uk/blog/airbnb-photography-boost-bookings-revenue) reports that professional photography boosts Airbnb bookings by an average of 24%. [Hospitable](https://hospitable.com/airbnb-photographer) notes that listings with professional photos also earn more wishlist saves — which feeds the algorithm's popularity signal.

What to prioritize in your photo set:
- **Cover photo:** Natural light, wide angle, the most aspirational space (usually the living room or a bedroom)
- **Lead with the experience, not the room:** Show the coffee nook, the reading corner, the view — guests book feelings, not square footage
- **Minimum 20 photos:** Cover every room, outdoor areas, key amenities, and neighborhood context
- **Update seasonally:** A cozy winter setup performs in December. A bright, airy layout performs in summer.

### 4. Set a Smart Minimum Stay Policy

Your minimum stay setting shapes your booking pattern more than most hosts realize. According to [Hostaway](https://www.hostaway.com/blog/airbnb-minimum-nights/), a blanket 2- or 3-night minimum creates "orphan gaps" in your calendar — single unbooked nights between reservations that no guest can fill.

The highest-performing approach is a **dynamic minimum stay strategy:**

- **Peak season and major events:** 3-7 night minimum — guests plan further ahead and are less price-sensitive
- **Shoulder season:** 2-night minimum — balances occupancy with revenue per booking
- **Off-season or slow periods:** 1-night minimum — fills gaps, attracts weekend travelers and last-minute bookings
- **Gaps under 3 nights:** Use a gap-fill rule in your pricing tool to automatically drop the minimum stay when a short gap appears between reservations

[Freewyld Foundry](https://freewyldfoundry.com/blog/minimum-night-stay/), which manages 3,800+ listings, identifies minimum stay misconfiguration as one of the top revenue-killing mistakes hosts make.

### 5. Enable Instant Book

Instant Book removes friction from the booking process. Guests who want a quick decision — especially last-minute travelers — consistently skip listings that require manual approval requests.

According to [PriceLabs](https://hello.pricelabs.co/blog/improve-airbnb-ranking-strategies/), enabling Instant Book also directly improves your search ranking because it signals reliability and increases Airbnb's confidence that a booking will complete without friction.

You can add pre-booking requirements (positive review history, government ID verification) to maintain guest quality while still offering the speed that converts browsers into bookers.

### 6. Choose the Right Cancellation Policy

A flexible cancellation policy increases bookings. An Expedia Group study cited by [PriceLabs](https://hello.pricelabs.co/blog/flexible-cancellation-policy/) found that flexible cancellation policies led to a 26% increase in gross bookings and a 53% rise in revenue. Young travelers in particular rate cancellation flexibility as a top booking factor — some report they would pay $50 more per night for it.

That does not mean offering unlimited free cancellations. The most practical approach for most hosts:

- **Moderate policy:** Full refund if canceled 5+ days before check-in. 50% refund of the nightly rate if canceled after that. This balances guest confidence with real calendar protection.
- **Firm policy:** Reserve this for peak periods and major events when demand is high and replacement bookings are easy to find.

An NBER research paper ([Jia, Jin & Wagman, 2022](https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w28878/w28878.pdf)) found that listings with more guest-friendly cancellation terms commanded higher prices *and* higher occupancy simultaneously — flexibility does not require a trade-off with revenue.

### 7. Protect Your Review Score

Review scores feed directly into the algorithm's quality signal and act as social proof for guests comparing listings at the same price point. A 4.7-star listing and a 4.9-star listing at the same price are not equal — in the algorithm or in the mind of the guest.

[Guesty](https://www.guesty.com/blog/airbnb-average-occupancy-rates/) identifies review scores and responsiveness as two of the most controllable factors in occupancy performance. To consistently earn 5-star ratings:

- Communicate proactively before arrival with check-in details, parking info, and local tips
- Set accurate expectations in your listing — overpromising leads to disappointment
- Respond to every review, positive or negative — it shows future guests you are engaged
- Address maintenance between every stay, not just when a guest reports a problem
- Leave a small personal touch at the property — a handwritten note goes a long way

A well-designed guest guidebook is one of the highest-ROI investments in your review score. When guests have clear instructions for WiFi, check-out, and house rules, they feel supported — and that feeling translates directly into 5-star ratings.

## A Real Example: High-Demand Event Markets

Occupancy strategy matters most when demand spikes are predictable. Hosts in major event markets who prepare their pricing and minimum stays months in advance consistently capture the premium that last-minute adjusters miss.

The LA28 Olympics and the FIFA World Cup are the two most significant demand events in the U.S. STR market this decade. Hosts in markets like Long Beach — where [19+ Olympic and Paralympic events are scheduled](https://longbeacholympics.com) — can apply the tactics in this article (dynamic pricing floors, extended minimums, firm cancellation policies) to capture 3-5x their normal nightly rates during event windows.

If you are in an LA-area market or host near any major sporting or cultural event, start building your event-pricing calendar now. Booking lead times for major events extend 6-12 months out, and the hosts who set their floors early capture the premium that reactive hosts leave behind.
